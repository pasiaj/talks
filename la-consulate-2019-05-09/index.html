Snapshots from Finnish Game Industry





Pasi Jokinen

1995

In May Finland won the Ice Hockey World Championships. In June we got funded. In August I was looking at my first paycheck, amazed that one can actually make money doing this.

Ilari Kuittinen, CEO of Housemarque, on getting their first publishing deal.

2002

We had a clear vision of the mobile gaming future. Nobody could have guessed it would take until 2008 for it to come true.

Markus Pasula, future founder of Grand Cru, about his time at Riot Entertainme-nt

2004

Four years of work in LA culmiated on one day in a root canal, swollen cheeck and a deal with Disney to create the Virtual Magic Kingdom.

Teemu Huuhtanen, future founder of Next Games, on his first signed deal in Hollywood.

2010

Nobody wanted to touch us. We had to beg people to come see what we had built. Finally we got one small publisher to take a look. They loved it. The word spread like wildfire overnight and the next morning there was a line at the door.

Samuli Syvähuoko, founder of Recoil Games, on getting a publishing deal after a failed project.

2012

I saw this simple free-to-play BMX game by Ben Hazard that had netted $3M. I knew I could make a better game. So I made Hill Climb Racing just as a quick test.

Toni Fingerroos, founder of Fingersoft. Hill Climb Racing has been downloaded more than 1 billion times.

2017

I was presenting our soft launch data to one of the biggest VCs in the industry. He told me: "You don't have a chance. You don't know anything about role playing games. You've never made a role playing game."

Timo Soininen, founder of Small Giant Games. Small Giant was sold to Zynga less than 2 years later for $560M.
